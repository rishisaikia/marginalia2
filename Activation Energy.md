## Definition
The minimum energy required to start a reaction or process. The initial barrier that must be overcome before change can occur.

## Key Insight
Starting is often harder than continuing. The first step requires the most effort. Once you overcome the initial activation energy, momentum carries you forward. Understanding this helps you design systems that lower the barrier to starting good behaviors and raise it for bad ones.

## How to Apply
For good habits, reduce activation energy:
- Make it easier to start (lay out workout clothes the night before)
- Reduce steps between decision and action
- Pre-commit to the first step, not the whole task

For bad habits, increase activation energy:
- Add friction (delete social media apps from phone)
- Create barriers between impulse and action

## Real-World Example
You want to go to the gym after work. High activation energy version: Drive home, change clothes, pack bag, drive to gym, find parking. Many decision points where you could quit. Low activation energy version: Pack gym bag the night before, bring it to work, go directly from work to gym without going home first. One decision, minimal friction.

A writer struggles with procrastination. High activation energy: Open computer, find document, figure out where you were, start writing. Lower activation energy: End each writing session mid-sentence. Next time, you just need to finish the thought—much easier to start.

Netflix autoplay reduces activation energy for watching another episode—no decision or action required, it just continues. This exploits your inertia. To reduce binge-watching, increase activation energy: Log out after each session, requiring deliberate login to continue.

Starting a business requires enormous activation energy. Quitting your job, getting funding, building product. Once started, continuing is easier than it was to start.

## Common Pitfalls
- Only focusing on starting, not building sustainable momentum
- Making activation energy so low you start things you shouldn't
- Not recognizing when high activation energy is actually protecting you from bad decisions
